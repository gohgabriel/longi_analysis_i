Chapter 1: Introduction

Why repeated measures need special treatment

Learning objectives
  • Understand why OLS fails for repeated-measures data
  • Define within- vs between-subject variance

Overview

Longitudinal data arises when the same subjects are observed at multiple time points. This structure — repeated measures on the same individual — violates the independence assumption of ordinary least squares regression, and ignoring it leads to incorrect standard errors and misleading inference.

The independence problem

OLS assumes that all observations are independent. In a longitudinal study, two measurements taken from the same person are almost certainly more similar to each other than two measurements from different people. This positive within-person correlation means OLS standard errors are too small, confidence intervals too narrow, and p-values too optimistic.

Key idea: The effective sample size for a longitudinal study is smaller than the raw number of observations, because observations cluster within individuals.

Within- vs between-subject variance

The total variance in a longitudinal outcome can be partitioned into two components:

  • Between-subject variance — how much individuals differ from each other on average (stable characteristics).
  • Within-subject variance — how much a given individual fluctuates over time around their own mean.

The intraclass correlation coefficient (ICC) quantifies what fraction of total variance is between subjects: 

ICC = σ²_between / (σ²_between + σ²_within)

An ICC near 1 means individuals are very consistent over time (most variance is between people). An ICC near 0 means individuals fluctuate a lot (most variance is within people).

What to do about it

The subsequent chapters cover three main families of approach:

  1. GLS / marginal models — model the covariance structure directly.
  2. Mixed-effects / multilevel models — add random effects for each individual.
  3. Bayesian multilevel models — place priors on variance components for regularised estimates.
